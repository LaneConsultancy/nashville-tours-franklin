## Executive Summary

### Key Findings

1. **Competitors are thin on content.** Franklin Walking Tours and Franklin on Foot homepages are both under 600 words. Tour pages average 150–250 words. This is a clear opportunity — Google rewards depth, and Diana's site can outrank with 3–5x the content.

2. **Ghost/haunted tour is the highest-demand product.** "Ghost tour Franklin TN" triggers a local pack, organic results, and discussion forums simultaneously. It is the highest-competition and highest-intent keyword in the market.

3. **Celebrity homes content is untapped by local tour operators.** The "celebrity homes Franklin TN" SERP is dominated by real estate blogs and social media, with no local tour company owning dedicated content. This is a major gap Diana can fill with a dedicated tour page.

4. **People search for Franklin TN tours from the Nashville context.** Related searches show "Franklin TN tours from Nashville" repeatedly — content should address proximity and the day-trip framing.

5. **FAQs are sparse across all competitors.** Franklin on Foot has 7 FAQs, Franklin Walking Tours has 6. Neither addresses pricing rationale, accessibility, group bookings, weather cancellation policies in depth, or the unique experience proposition. Diana can lead with a comprehensive FAQ section.

## Part 9: Content Structure Recommendations for Tour Pages

### The "Tour Page Formula" That Converts

Based on analysis of top-performing tour pages (Walk Eat Nashville, US Ghost Adventures, Gray Line Tennessee), the following structure converts best:

```
1. Keyword-optimised H1 headline
2. Hero image (actual tour in action, not stock)
3. Quick-scan overview box (duration / price / group size / meeting point / difficulty)
4. Compelling opening paragraph — emotional hook, what makes this tour special
5. "What you'll experience" — 4–6 bullet points or mini-sections with specifics
6. Guide/host section — humanises the experience, builds trust
7. "This tour is perfect for..." — audience identification; visitors self-select in
8. Practical information — what to bring, accessibility, weather policy
9. Testimonials — minimum 3, tour-specific
10. FAQ section — 6–8 questions, reduce booking friction
11. Booking CTA — prominent, repeated at top and bottom
12. Related tours — internal link to 1–2 other tours
```

### Word Density Note

Top-performing tour pages by Walk Eat Nashville and US Ghost Adventures are 600–1,000 words. All Franklin TN local competitors are 150–350 words per tour. Diana's pages at 800–1,200 words will be the most content-rich in the market — this is the primary SEO lever available without backlink investment.

### Schema Markup Recommendations

Implement the following structured data on each tour page:
- `TouristAttraction` — for all tour pages
- `Event` (with recurring event support) — for regularly scheduled tours
- `FAQPage` — for FAQ sections
- `LocalBusiness` — on homepage and contact page
- `Review` / `AggregateRating` — where reviews are displayed

### Internal Linking Structure

Each tour page should link to:
- 1–2 related tour pages ("Guests who love this also enjoy...")
- The booking/contact page
- The About page (first mention of Diana)
- The homepage (breadcrumb)

The homepage should link to all individual tour pages with keyword-rich anchor text.
