## Opening

Franklin, Tennessee has been called Music City's best-kept secret, which is a little ironic given the names on the property records.

Keith Urban and Nicole Kidman. Carrie Underwood. Reese Witherspoon. Brad Paisley and Kimberly Williams-Paisley. Kenny Chesney. Alan Jackson — whose former 135-acre estate became one of the most talked-about property sales in Williamson County history. The Cyrus family. Kathie Lee Gifford. Amy Grant.

This is where country music royalty, Hollywood A-listers, and the crossover between them have quietly built their lives. Not in Nashville proper — in Franklin, twenty miles south, where the estates have room to breathe and the town is small enough that the most famous person at the coffee shop is occasionally just someone's neighbor.

The question most people ask is: why Franklin? The answer is interesting enough that it occupies a significant portion of this tour. But the short version is: privacy, space, proximity to Nashville without Nashville's pace, and a community that has an unspoken agreement about letting people be people. A lot of enormous talent has chosen Franklin because Franklin lets them choose it without consequence.

Nashville Tours Franklin is the only local tour company with a dedicated tour of Franklin's celebrity homes and filming locations. We cover the estates, the neighborhoods, the spots where stars have been photographed going about their actual lives, and the downtown streets that keep appearing in Hallmark movies because they look exactly like what they are: an almost impossibly picturesque American small town.

## Filming Locations — Franklin's Hallmark Town Status

### H2: Why Franklin Keeps Appearing on Screen

Franklin, Tennessee has been called a "Hallmark town," and the label has stuck for good reason. The downtown historic district looks like the town that every feel-good movie has been trying to construct on a backlot for decades — except it's real, it's been there for over two centuries, and it doesn't require set dressing because it simply looks the way it looks.

The connection runs deeper than the architecture. Kimberly Williams-Paisley — who is both a Franklin resident and a working actress — has starred in Hallmark content with direct Franklin connections. The Sister Swap films brought the Hallmark audience's attention to Franklin in a way that matched what travel bloggers had been saying for years: this town is absurdly photogenic.

Diana covers the specific locations in downtown Franklin that have appeared in productions — the storefronts, the public spaces, the Main Street blocks that photograph like a postcard but are also fully functioning parts of a real town. For guests who've encountered Franklin through a screen and come to see it in person, this portion of the tour tends to be particularly satisfying.

## An Important Note on Privacy

### H2: Drive-By, Not Walk-Up

This tour operates entirely on public roads. We drive past properties; we do not enter private driveways, approach private gates, or encourage any contact with residents or their staff. This is non-negotiable — not because we're required to say it, but because it's the right way to operate in a community where people have chosen to live precisely because it respects their privacy.

Diana's approach is to give you the genuine story and context behind each property — who lives there, what brought them here, what their connection to Franklin is — not to manufacture a moment of intrusion. The knowledge is more interesting than a close-up photograph anyway.

## Why Franklin?

### H2: What Draws the Biggest Names in the Business Here

The short answer: Nashville without Nashville.

The music industry infrastructure in Nashville is the reason so many artists are in the area at all. But the city itself has grown considerably, and the neighborhoods closest to the industry have become both expensive and visible. Franklin offers the other side of that equation: large properties on substantial acreage, a genuinely small-town atmosphere, excellent schools, a food and arts scene that has developed to serve an affluent residential base, and a 30-minute drive to Nashville when you need it.

There's also the Williamson County effect more broadly — the combination of privacy and community that seems to appeal specifically to people who want a real life alongside a significant public career. The celebrities who've settled here tend to stay, and their presence has attracted more of the same.
